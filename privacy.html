Privacy Policy

Call Sentinel is built with one principle: your calls are your business.

We do not see, collect, transmit, store, or process any call data from your device. Ever.

All call blocking, filtering, and detection happens entirely on your phone. There are no servers. No cloud. No tracking.

What data does Call Sentinel collect?

None.

Call Sentinel does not collect personal data, call logs, phone numbers, contacts, or any user information.

How does the app work without servers?

The app uses Android’s built-in call screening and blocking capabilities directly on your device. Decisions are made locally and never leave your phone.

Do you store any information?

No. There is no account system, no cloud sync, and no external storage of any kind.

Do you share data with third parties?

No. Because we never have access to any data in the first place.

FAQ

Why does the app need call permissions?
Android requires these permissions for any app that wants to block or screen incoming calls. The permissions are used locally on your device only.

Can you see who calls me?
No. We have zero visibility into your calls.

Is my data sent to the internet?
No. Call Sentinel works entirely offline.

Does Call Sentinel use analytics or tracking?
No analytics. No tracking. No telemetry.
